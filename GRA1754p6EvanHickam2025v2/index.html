Flowers

Coleus

The coleus plant is known for its vibrant, multicolored foliage and thrives in warm, humid climates. It grows well in South Florida because it loves heat, can tolerate partial shade, and adapts easily to the region’s moist soil. Its fast growth and low maintenance make it a popular choice for adding color to gardens and landscapes year-round.

Salvia

Salvia is a hardy, flowering plant that produces bright, tubular blooms that attract pollinators like bees and hummingbirds. It grows well in South Florida thanks to its tolerance for heat, humidity, and sandy, well-drained soil. With its long blooming season and drought resistance, salvia is an excellent choice for low-maintenance, colorful landscapes.

Wax Begonia

Wax begonia is a compact, bushy plant with glossy leaves and delicate flowers in shades of pink, red, or white. It thrives in South Florida’s warm, humid climate and performs well in both sun and partial shade. Its resilience, low water needs, and year-round blooming make it a favorite for borders, containers, and garden beds.

Herbs

Basil

Basil is a fragrant, leafy herb known for its bright green leaves and sweet, peppery flavor, commonly used in cooking. It thrives in South Florida because the region’s warm temperatures, abundant sunlight, and high humidity create ideal growing conditions. Basil grows quickly in well-drained soil, common in South Florida gardens and containers.

Ginger

Ginger is a tropical plant with slender green stems and broad, glossy leaves, prized for its aromatic, spicy root used in cooking and medicine. It flourishes in South Florida due to the area's consistently warm temperatures, high humidity, and frequent rainfall, which mimic the plant's native tropical habitat. Ginger also prefers well-drained, fertile soil, making it well-suited to the rich, moist conditions found in South Florida's gardens.

Rosemary

Rosemary is an aromatic herb with needle-like leaves and a strong, piney scent, commonly used to enhance the flavor of meats, vegetables, and stews. It grows well in South Florida because it thrives in the region’s sunny, warm climate and well-drained, slightly sandy soils. Rosemary also tolerates occasional drought, making it perfect for South Florida’s conditions, where it can withstand periods of lower rainfall while still flourishing.

Vegetables

Sweet Potato

Sweet potato is a fast-growing vine with heart-shaped leaves and underground tubers that are rich in nutrients and naturally sweet. It thrives in South Florida’s long growing season, warm temperatures, and sandy, well-drained soil. The plant also handles heat and humidity well, making it a reliable and productive crop for South Florida gardens.

Hot Peppers

Hot peppers are vibrant, fruit-bearing plants that produce spicy pods in a variety of shapes, sizes, and colors, often used to add heat and flavor to dishes. They grow exceptionally well in South Florida thanks to the region’s warm temperatures, abundant sunshine, and extended growing season. Hot peppers also prefer well-drained soil and can tolerate periods of drought, making them a strong match for South Florida’s climate.

Tropical Spinach

Tropical spinaches, like Okinawa spinach and Malabar spinach, are leafy greens known for their vibrant color, mild flavor, and heat tolerance. They thrive in South Florida’s hot, humid climate where traditional spinach struggles, making them an excellent warm-weather alternative. These hardy plants grow quickly in well-drained soil and can be harvested continuously, providing a steady supply of nutritious greens year-round.
